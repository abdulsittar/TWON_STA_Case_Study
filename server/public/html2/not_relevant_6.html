Serbia Signs Free Trade Agreement with China to Boost Exports
Vesti iz sveta

Serbia has officially signed a free trade agreement (FTA) with China, a move expected to significantly boost Serbian exports and attract Chinese investment. The agreement, which eliminates tariffs on a wide range of goods, is set to take effect in 2025 and will open new opportunities for Serbian businesses in one of the world's largest markets.

One of the key sectors expected to benefit from this deal is agriculture. Serbian fruit producers, particularly those exporting raspberries, apples, and plums, will now have easier access to the Chinese market without facing high import taxes. Additionally, Serbia's growing wine and dairy industries are expected to gain new customers in China, where demand for high-quality European food products is on the rise.

Beyond agriculture, the free trade agreement also facilitates industrial exports. Serbian manufacturers in the automotive, machinery, and electronics sectors are expected to expand their operations with fewer restrictions. China, in return, will have better access to Serbian markets for technology, infrastructure projects, and consumer goods. The agreement aligns with Serbia's ongoing economic strategy of strengthening ties with non-EU countries while maintaining strong trade relations with the European Union.

While officials in Belgrade celebrate the deal as a major economic win, some critics warn of potential risks. Increased Chinese imports could put pressure on local Serbian businesses, and analysts urge the government to ensure fair competition. Additionally, Serbia must balance its growing economic ties with China while keeping EU accession negotiations on track, as Brussels has raised concerns about China's increasing influence in the Balkans.
