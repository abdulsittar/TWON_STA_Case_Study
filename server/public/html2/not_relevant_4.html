IMF Warns of Fiscal Risks Over Serbia's Expo and National Stadium Projects
Danas info

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has raised concerns about the growing fiscal risks in Serbia due to increased government spending on major infrastructure projects, particularly Expo 2027 and the construction of a National Stadium. The IMF warns that these projects, while potentially beneficial in the long term, lack transparency and could strain Serbia's public finances.

The Serbian government has been heavily investing in these large-scale projects, arguing that they will boost tourism, create jobs, and improve the country's international reputation. However, critics worry about the costs and whether these investments will yield the expected economic benefits.

Expo 2027 is expected to attract millions of visitors, and the government hopes it will generate significant revenue. However, past experiences with international expos suggest that returns on investment are not always guaranteed. Meanwhile, the construction of a National Stadium, estimated to cost hundreds of millions of euros, has been controversial, with many questioning whether such an expensive project is necessary when other infrastructure needs are more pressing.

The IMF has urged Serbia to adopt stricter fiscal policies and ensure more transparency in public spending to avoid potential economic instability.
